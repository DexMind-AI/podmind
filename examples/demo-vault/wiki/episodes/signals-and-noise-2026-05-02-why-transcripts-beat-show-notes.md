🎧 [[shows/signals-and-noise]] — Tomas Ek built a search engine over his own listening history — and found the real blocker was transcripts, not search.

## Key takeaways

- Only ~1 in 50 podcast feeds publishes a real transcript; show notes are marketing copy and you can't search aspirations.
- His transcript cascade: published RSS transcript first, YouTube auto-captions second, local Whisper overnight as the last resort (~a third of episodes).
- The economics flipped around 2024 when consumer laptops began transcribing faster than real time — the hard part became plumbing, not compute.
- Once episodes become greppable pages, listening stops being ephemeral; he annotates claims and tracks which guests turned out to be right.
- The transcript isn't the product — the accumulated cross-references are.

## Notable quotes

> Show notes tell you what the producer hoped the episode was about; the transcript tells you what was actually said. — Tomas Ek (00:02:40)
