🎧 [[shows/signals-and-noise]] — Priya Nair on why a folder of markdown files outlives every SaaS knowledge tool — and why agents make plain text the power move.

## Key takeaways

- Notes that can't survive the company that made the editor were never your notes — a deprecated export feature turns them into hostages.
- A personal knowledge base has one user and a fifty-year horizon; no vendor roadmap survives that, but a folder of text files already has.
- Plain text lets a language model be a full citizen of the wiki — reading and writing every page through the filesystem, no API keys required.
- SaaS AI sidebars see one document at a time; a local agent with grep sees the whole graph.
- Honest costs of local-first: mobile is genuinely worse and links rot on careless renames — weekend problems, versus the decade problem of lock-in.
